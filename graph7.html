Hedonic Price Index

Henry Wu

The line graph shows the recent changes in Chines Aritfacts hedonic price index. The hedonic price index is an index based on the changes in the hedonic price or hedonic regression, and hedonic regression in economics is defined as the estimation of the monetary value of an object by using related characterized baskets of goods. In this case, 2000 will be the base year.

Clearly, modern and late Qing artifacts showed a very similar pattern. Between 2000 and 2003, the price index remained low and even fell by a tiny proportion. Surprisingly, the price went up dramatically to almost 1.5 from 2003 to 2005 but quickly decreased to just below 1 between 2005 and 2010. However, the price index reached a peak of over 1.5 for late Qing and Modern artifacts in 2011. Finally, the price index went down gradually to below 1.5 after 2011.

On the other hand, the price change was much lower for ancient artifacts. Between 2000 and 2003, there were large fluctuations between -1 and 0. Then, it rose to 0.5 in 2005 and 2006 but fell back to 0 afterward. After 2008, the price index increased to over 0.5 yet it fell back to 0 again in 2017.
